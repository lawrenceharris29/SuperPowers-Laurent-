# English Voice Enrollment Passages
**Purpose:** Capture speaker timbre, cadence, and prosodic baseline for TTS transfer
**Total Duration:** ~10-12 minutes (2.5-3 min each)
**Instructions:** Record each passage in a single take. Maintain consistent microphone distance. Breathe naturally.

---

## Passage 1: News Anchor / Neutral Informative
*Target: Broadcast clarity, declarative rhythm, steady pace*

The International Space Station continues its decades-long mission as a testament to multinational cooperation in low Earth orbit. Earlier this morning, a resupply craft successfully docked, delivering nearly three tons of scientific equipment, provisions, and replacement hardware. Engineers on the ground monitored telemetry data while astronauts performed routine checks on life support systems. The mission represents the forty-second commercial resupply service conducted by private aerospace contractors working in partnership with national agencies. Scientists hope the new laboratory modules will accelerate research into crystalline protein structures and advanced water purification technologies. Weather patterns at the launch site remained favorable throughout the four-hour launch window, with ground temperatures holding steady at sixty-eight degrees Fahrenheit and wind speeds below twelve knots.

## Passage 2: Casual Storytelling / Conversational
*Target: Natural rhythm, contractions, varied intonation, personal connection*

So I was walking through the park last Tuesday—totally forgot my umbrella, of course—and it starts drizzling out of nowhere. There's this coffee stand near the lake that I'd never noticed before, run by this elderly guy with these incredibly intricate tattoos on his forearms. Anyway, he waves me over, offers me this tiny cup of something he calls "mountain drip," and we just start talking about his grandkids, about woodworking, about how the neighborhood's changed since the eighties. By the time the rain stopped, I'd been standing there for forty minutes, completely soaked but weirdly happy about it. Sometimes the best conversations happen when you're just trying to get out of the weather, you know? I went back yesterday and he remembered my order.

## Passage 3: Excited / Enthusiastic
*Target: Elevated pitch, shorter phrases, dynamic range, exclamatory patterns*

We got it! After three months of absolute chaos—deadlines, redesigns, endless debugging—the prototype actually works! Look at these metrics! The processing speed is forty percent faster than our initial projections, and the energy consumption dropped to nearly half! I literally cannot believe we pulled this off. The team crushed it, absolutely crushed it. Wait until the board sees this demonstration. They're going to flip! We need to celebrate—tonight, somewhere loud with terrible music and excellent appetizers. This changes everything for the product line. Call everyone! Text the investors! We have officially entered new territory here, and it feels incredible!

## Passage 4: Calm / Empathetic / Reassuring
*Target: Slower tempo, warm resonance, gentle onsets, sustained vowels*

I know this moment feels overwhelming, and that's completely okay. Take a breath with me... in... and out. You're not alone in this, even when it feels like the weight is too much to carry. Every challenge you've faced before has prepared you for right now, even if you can't see how yet. There's no rush to figure everything out today. Small steps count. Rest is productive, too. When you're ready, we'll look at this together, piece by piece, without judgment or hurry. You have time. You have support. The difficulty you're feeling now isn't permanent, even though I know it feels endless. Trust that ease will return, slowly, like tide coming back to shore.
